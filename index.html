NASA's Webb Telescope: Searching for Young Brown Dwarfs and Rogue Planets

The James Webb Space Telescope is set to explore young brown dwarfs and rogue planets in our galaxy.

This mission aims to deepen our understanding of these celestial objects that exist between stars and planets.

Brown dwarfs, often called "failed stars," do not sustain hydrogen fusion in their cores.

Rogue planets, on the other hand, float freely in space without a host star.

Webb's advanced instruments will provide unprecedented insights into their formation and characteristics.
